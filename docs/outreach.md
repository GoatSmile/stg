# Outreach — the intro and the email

*The warm-intro sequence to Yulia Lyusina (SVP Strategy, Transformation &
Sustainability). Placeholders in [brackets] — owner fills. Strategy:
[ceo-play.md](ceo-play.md) §5. Tone: peer-to-peer, low-pressure, zero hype.
Keep it short; the video does the work.*

---

## Step 1 — what the wife says to Yulia (verbal / Teams / short email)

Internal colleague, casual, no commitment asked beyond "watch a 3-min video."
She is the credibility bridge — she should not pitch, just vouch and tee up.

> **Verbal / Teams:**
> "Hi Yulia — totally outside my lane, but my husband [name] builds AI tools
> for a living, and he got a bit obsessed with the regulatory pressure on our
> categories — the France ban, the EU tax stuff. He built a working thing, in
> his own time, that turns those into a P&L view on our own footprint, all from
> public data. He'll send you a 3-minute video — no meeting, no deck.
> Worth a look if you have a coffee's worth of time. Can I tell him to email
> you?"

Goal: Yulia expects the email and has pre-agreed to watch. That single yes is
the whole game.

## Step 2 — the email (sent by the owner, after the wife's nod)

Subject lines (**recommend B** — it leads with the warm-intro equity; A/C lead
with the issue, which from an unknown sender reads as a vendor who's already
decided what your problem is):
- **B (recommended):** "[wife] suggested I send you this — 3 minutes, no deck"
- **A:** "The EU tax revision, as a number on STG's P&L (3-min video)"
- **C:** "What COM(2025) 580 is worth to STG — a working demo"

> Hi Yulia,
>
> [wife] mentioned I'd be in touch. I build AI software for a living
> ([valent.dk]), and I built a working prototype that sits squarely in your
> world.
>
> Every regulatory event hitting tobacco and nicotine right now — the EU's
> proposed tax revision, the France ban, the Danish flavour cap — lands on STG
> as a number. Today that number gets worked out by hand, late. The tool turns
> a specific regulatory event — say the EU tax revision — into a DKK EBITDA band
> on STG's own footprint, with every assumption visible and editable, and every
> figure citing its source (it refuses to guess when there isn't one).
>
> Here's a 3-minute walkthrough — me, talking you through it, no slides:
> **[video link]**
>
> One thing up front: it's built only from STG's published filings and public
> regulatory texts — nothing from [wife], nothing internal — and it's framed
> strictly as internal scenario prep, not anything investor-facing. Happy to
> have your Legal team look at it. It's a model of the *shape* of the impact;
> plug in real volumes and it gets sharp.
>
> If it's useful, reply and I'll tailor it to STG's actual numbers and walk you
> through it live. If not, no follow-up — you'll have lost three minutes.
>
> Best,
> [name]
> [valent.dk] · [phone]

**Notes:**
- ~190 words. One link. One ask (reply). No attachment.
- Frame is **internal decision-support / scenario prep** — never investor copy
  (EU MAR discipline). No mention of share price, earnings calls, or IR. The
  explicit "nothing from [wife], nothing internal… happy to have Legal look"
  line pre-empts the real MAR reflex (an external party circulating a DKK figure
  on a listed issuer via an employee's spouse) and converts it into a trust
  signal.
- "Working prototype," not "weekend project" — humility in tone, not in a word
  that caps how seriously an ex-BCG reader can take the artifact.
- The tool models a *curated* set of events; the email says "a specific
  regulatory event," not "any live text" — don't promise live-parsing the build
  deliberately doesn't do (she might reply "run it on yesterday's TRIS notice").
- The video link is in the body so the email forwards cleanly to the CFO; the
  video itself contains no link (see [demo-script.md](demo-script.md)).
- The "no follow-up if not" line lowers the cost of saying yes and signals
  confidence — calibrated for an ex-BCG reader who dislikes being sold to.
- Don't name-drop the CEO/CFO. Let the artifact travel on its own merit.

## Step 3 — if she replies

- Offer a 20-min live session: same demo, but you type in a real STG figure she
  gives you (or a published one she trusts) so the band moves on *her* number.
- Bring the **honest-denominator** ask: "the only thing standing between
  analyst-grade and finance-grade is your real volumes behind a simple data
  agreement." That's the bridge to the paid phase.
- Let her decide who else sees it. Do not pre-empt her by forwarding upward
  yourself.
