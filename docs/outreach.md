**Notes:**
- ~190 words. One link. One ask (reply). No attachment.
- Frame is **internal decision-support / scenario prep** — never investor copy
  (EU MAR discipline). No mention of share price, earnings calls, or IR. The
  explicit "nothing from [wife], nothing internal… happy to have Legal look"
  line pre-empts the real MAR reflex (an external party circulating a DKK figure
  on a listed issuer via an employee's spouse) and converts it into a trust
  signal.
- "Working prototype," not "weekend project" — humility in tone, not in a word
  that caps how seriously an ex-BCG reader can take the artifact.
- The tool models a *curated* set of events; the email says "a specific
  regulatory event," not "any live text" — don't promise live-parsing the build
  deliberately doesn't do (she might reply "run it on yesterday's TRIS notice").
- The video link is in the body so the email forwards cleanly to the CFO; the
  video itself contains no link (see [demo-script.md](demo-script.md)).
- The "no follow-up if not" line lowers the cost of saying yes and signals
  confidence — calibrated for an ex-BCG reader who dislikes being sold to.
- Don't name-drop the CEO/CFO. Let the artifact travel on its own merit.
