## Step 1 — what the wife says to Yulia (verbal / Teams / short email)

Internal colleague, casual, no commitment asked beyond "watch a 3-min video."
She is the credibility bridge — she should not pitch, just vouch and tee up.

> **Verbal / Teams:**
> "Hi Yulia — totally outside my lane, but my husband [name] builds AI tools
> for a living, and he got a bit obsessed with the regulatory pressure on our
> categories — the France ban, the EU tax stuff. He built a little working
> thing over a weekend that turns those into a P&L view on our own footprint,
> all from public data. He'll send you a 3-minute video — no meeting, no deck.
> Worth a look if you have a coffee's worth of time. Can I tell him to email
> you?"

Goal: Yulia expects the email and has pre-agreed to watch. That single yes is
the whole game.

## Step 2 — the email (sent by the owner, after the wife's nod)

Subject lines (pick one — A/B by gut):
- **A:** "The EU tax revision, as a number on STG's P&L (3-min video)"
- **B:** "[wife] suggested I send you this — 3 minutes, no deck"
- **C:** "What COM(2025)580 is worth to STG — a working demo"

> Hi Yulia,
>
> [wife] mentioned I'd be in touch. I build AI software for a living
> ([valent.dk]), and I spent a weekend on something that sits squarely in your
> world.
>
> Every regulatory event hitting tobacco and nicotine right now — the EU tax

> Today that number gets worked out by hand, late, and usually after the

> every assumption visible and editable, and every figure citing its source (it
> refuses to guess when there isn't one).
>
> Here's a 3-minute walkthrough — me, talking you through it, no slides:
> **[video link]**
>
> It's built entirely on STG's published disclosures and public regulatory
> texts — zero internal data. That's the point: it's a model of the *shape* of
> the impact. Plug in real volumes and it gets sharp.
>
> If it's useful, reply and I'll wire it to STG's actual numbers and walk you
> through it live. If not, no follow-up — you'll have lost three minutes.
>
> Best,
> [name]
> [valent.dk] · [phone]

**Notes:**
- ~170 words. One link. One ask (reply). No attachment.
- Frame is **internal decision-support / scenario prep** — never investor copy
  (EU MAR discipline). No mention of share price, earnings calls, or IR.
- The video link is in the body so the email forwards cleanly to the CFO; the
  video itself contains no link (see [demo-script.md](demo-script.md)).
- The "no follow-up if not" line lowers the cost of saying yes and signals
  confidence — calibrated for an ex-BCG reader who dislikes being sold to.
- Don't name-drop the CEO/CFO. Let the artifact travel on its own merit.

## Step 3 — if she replies

- Offer a 20-min live session: same demo, but you type in a real STG figure she
  gives you (or a published one she trusts) so the band moves on *her* number.
- Bring the **honest-denominator** ask: "the only thing standing between
  analyst-grade and finance-grade is your real volumes behind a simple data
  agreement." That's the bridge to the paid phase.
- Let her decide who else sees it. Do not pre-empt her by forwarding upward
  yourself.
