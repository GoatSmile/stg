# Video script — the 3-minute walkthrough

*The email payload. ~3 min (hard ceiling 4). Owner to camera, intercut with
screen share. Worked example: the **EU tobacco-tax revision** (hits the 50%
combustible core — Yulia's strategy wheelhouse; France is the alternate, see
end). The app is now **complete — 7 department lenses, 5 live public-data feeds**
— so the "genuinely live" beat is real, not a mockup. Spoken ≈ 470 words
(~150 wpm). Strategy: [ceo-play.md](ceo-play.md) §5b.*

**Recording notes:**
- Screen-record at 1920×1080, cursor visible, **clean browser window** (no
  personal bookmarks bar / tabs), **light mode** (the parchment theme — the hero).
- **Pre-warm the live feeds first**: open HR, Finance, Procurement, Supply and
  ESG once so each feed's cache is hot, then they render instantly on camera. The
  feeds are genuinely live; pre-warming just avoids a cold-fetch pause. (Offline
  `DEMO_MODE` golden is the fallback if wifi dies — but live is the wow now.)
- **Gate OFF** while recording — don't set `SITE_PASSWORD` locally or you'll hit
  your own password wall.
- **Record the VO after the build, and say the actual on-screen numbers** — the
  DKK band and the open-roles count are live output; read what's on screen, don't
  pre-commit a figure in narration.
- Talk like you're sitting next to her. No music. One take is fine — warmth beats
  polish. (The app is quietly built in STG's own visual language; let that land on
  its own — don't point it out.)

> "Hi Yulia — I'm [name], [wife]'s husband; she said she'd give you a heads-up. I

### 0:20–0:50 — the platform (the map)

> "This is STG's footprint — built only on your published annual report, nothing

*Click the EU threat → Impact Room.*

### 0:50–2:00 — the money-shot (Impact Room)

> "This is the proposed EU tax revision — COM(2025) 580, still in Council — which
> would raise excise minimums on cigars and pipe from 2028. It models only the
> slice of your European cigar-and-pipe base that sits *below* the new floor —
> you'd tell me which markets that's actually true in; I've made a transparent
> assumption. The question nobody answers cleanly today is: what's it worth to
> STG?
>
> The answer is a band, not a fake single number — and right up here it says what
> it is: a public-data model, not STG's own figure. And the part I care about most
> — these are *assumptions*, and they're yours to argue with."

*Drag the assumption sliders — the band moves.*

> "How much of the tax passes to the consumer? How sensitive is demand — and note
> it says plainly there's no clean public elasticity for cigars, so this is a
> range *you* set, not a borrowed number. Drag it, the band moves, the model shows
> its working. Every figure carries its source —" *point to a citation chip* "—
> and where the source is silent, like a France-specific line you don't publish,
> it says so, right here," *point to the abstention* "instead of inventing it. It
> would rather abstain than be wrong in front of you. That honesty is the whole
> design — this is a scenario you can take into a room, not a forecast I'm asking
> you to trust blindly."

*Switch back to the map.*

### 2:00–2:40 — the live proof (the platform is live)

*Cut to camera.*

### 2:40–3:00 — to camera, the close

> "That's it. It's deliberately cheap and fast to stand up — so you can

*End. No outro card with a link — the link lives in the email so the video
forwards clean.*

## Recovery lines (if a number or claim is challenged)

- **"That figure looks off."** → "It's deliberately a model on public splits, not
  your real volumes — that's exactly the gap I'd close with your data. Tell me the
  right number and watch the band move."
- **"Is the live data actually real?"** → "Yes — the open roles, the freight cost,
  the leaf-region climate and water stress, the FX are all pulled from public
  sources, each with its source link on screen. The *regulatory* side is a
  curated, dated corpus, not a live web scrape — deliberately, so it never invents
  a number."
- **"We already track regulation."** → "You track *that it's happening*. This is
  what it's *worth* — to STG specifically, in kroner, with the assumptions on the
  table. I haven't found anyone who sells exactly that — the closest is a ~$4k
  static spreadsheet or six-figure bespoke consulting."
- **"Is this an IR / investor tool?"** → "No — strictly internal scenario prep. I
  deliberately don't generate anything investor-facing."

## Alternate worked example — France (swap for 0:50–2:00)

If leading with France instead: it's more acute (ban in force April 2026, Conseil
d'État merits ruling due this month) and dual — it removes a pouch market *and*
threatens the ~DKK 620m machine-rolled cigar market (France is STG's #1). Use it
if the goal is urgency over strategic scale; use EU excise (above) if the goal is
the bigger long-horizon number Yulia owns. **Don't do both in the video — one
clean scenario only.**
