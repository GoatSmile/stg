# Varsel — commercial strategy

*How to commercialise Varsel with STG, and whether there's a product beyond them.*
*Compiled 2026-06-18 from a six-advisor panel (consulting-pricing · vertical-SaaS pricing · STG buyer-side/procurement · Danish commercial-legal · vertical-SaaS VC · a designated skeptic). Figures are reasoned negotiation anchors, not quotes — see §9.*

---

## 1. The reframe — it's one ladder, not two doors

"Consulting per hour" vs "a product I licence" is a false choice. All six advisors converged on the same structure:

> **Lead with consulting, contract to keep the engine, let the platform accrete underneath.** The prototype's only job is to convert the warm intro into *one paid, fixed-fee engagement on STG's real numbers*. Every contract is written so the Varsel engine stays valent.dk's IP. The "product" is the asset you build up under the engagements and licence later — never sold as software to STG on day one.

Why a solo with a warm intro but no enterprise track record **cannot** sell a DKK 9bn cost-cutting listed issuer a licence/retainer on first contact: it triggers vendor procurement, infosec review, a DPIA, and a "who maintains this if you're hit by a bus" gate a one-person shop fails on paper. What STG *can* buy cheaply, off an existing advisory budget, is **a person who turns a regulation into a defensible, cited DKK band, fast.** Consulting is the only landable-now revenue; the platform is the asset accreted underneath. The hybrid is the only structure where each path de-risks the other — consulting funds 2026 and proves the method on real data; the retained-IP engine means you finance a platform with STG's money instead of selling hours forever.

The honesty/citation discipline — *every number cites a public source or abstains* — is the rare differentiator the buyer-panel already validated. It is both the moat and the liability shield, and it is what you're actually selling: **judgement, not software.**

## 3. The two options, head to head

### Consulting — the only path that yields a signed invoice in 2026
- **Structure:** productized **fixed-fee** scoped deliverables under an ApS — *not* time-and-materials. Day rate (DKK 12–18k) used only to cost scope and set a named change-order rate internally; never the headline.
- **Figures:** pilot 95–150k · DPA build 250–600k · bespoke deep-dive 150–400k each · retainer 300–600k/yr.
- ✅ Landable now; dodges procurement under threshold; fixed-fee escapes the day-rate commodity trap; each rung re-uses the engine you keep. Low-ops to run solo (JSON-first, cached feeds).
- ❌ Single-customer concentration (100% STG = a fragile job, not a business, at a buyer mid-downturn); key-person/bus-factor caps the ceiling; the "data's public, our FP&A can do this" objection your own demo invites; the hours-trap if you ever drift to T&M.

### Product / platform — the asset, not a day-one sale
- **Structure:** single-tenant, **hosted by you, never ship the repo.** Tiered: Base (public-data) → +Scenarios → Real-data/SKU premium. Every licence paired with a one-time implementation fee (30–60% of first-year ARR — standard).
- **Figures:** single-enterprise licence **250–450k/yr ARR + 100–250k implementation**; post-DPA real-data tier **+300–600k/yr + 200–400k integration** → full relationship **600k–1m+/yr**. Per-logo to other firms 200–600k/yr.
- ✅ Recurring, IP-retained, decouples revenue from hours; genuine verified whitespace; honesty discipline is a hard-to-commoditise moat that travels.
- ❌ Unsellable as software to STG day-one; tobacco is reputationally radioactive (VC-uninvestable); half the value (STG footprint model + tobacco corpus) isn't portable → scales like a **boutique (3–6 logos), not horizontal SaaS**; building multi-tenancy before customer #2 is the founder graveyard.

## 5. IP & deal structure — the load-bearing section

> **The IP clause is the entire platform business.** One work-for-hire / total-assignment sentence in a standard STG MSA and you've sold the product for consulting wages.

- **Incorporate Valent ApS this week** (capital DKK 20,000 — can sit in the company; setup ~DKK 0–5k) and **assign the existing Varsel codebase + regulatory corpus + model library into it**, so it demonstrably *pre-exists* any STG contract. This makes everything STG touches "licensed Background IP," not "work made for the Customer," and turns you from "a guy with a demo" into "a Danish company that licenses a product." An *enkeltmandsvirksomhed* co-mingles you and the asset — don't. (VAT/moms 25% on B2B Danish invoices is cash-flow-neutral to STG; not a price point.)
- **Contract each job as a Danish konsulentaftale** (Danish law, venue Copenhagen) with the IP clause in §Appendix B: you **retain** all pre-existing/independently-developed IP (engine, model templates, methodology, citation rails, corpus); STG gets a **non-exclusive, non-transferable licence** to the delivered configured instance and its **outputs**; STG-specific config is "Customer Configuration Data" they may keep; all enhancements/derivatives/generalisations of the software vest in Consultant. **Strike** any "samtlige immaterielle rettigheder til leverancen overgår til kunden" / work-for-hire language. Deliverable = "a report + a hosted licensed instance," never "the software."
- **The moment real STG data enters:** a GDPR **Databehandleraftale** (art. 28) — STG controller, you processor, EU hosting (Supabase is already eu-west-3), sub-processor list, breach notification, deletion on termination, and a clause that aggregated/derived **model improvements containing no STG confidential data remain yours** (how you legally learn from the engagement). Compute-and-return bands; keep raw volumes firewalled (minimises processor liability + MAR surface).
- **Bus-factor without giving up IP:** a source-code **escrow** clause (release to STG only on your insolvency/cessation). **Cap liability at the fee value**; carry **erhvervsansvarsforsikring (E&O)** — tobacco + financial-impact claims are exposure you cannot personally absorb.
- **Exclusivity trap:** STG may ask for tobacco/nicotine sector-exclusivity "since it's our data." Concede at most **time-boxed + paid** (e.g. 12 months, +50–100% fee) — never perpetual, never free.
- **Hygiene:** disclose the HR-intro relationship proactively and keep your wife out of the commercial loop (so no one can call it nepotistic procurement). Every output is internal decision-support, never investor-facing (**MAR**); accept an insider-list listing if asked.

## 6. Is there a general product? Verdict: **pursue it — but only after STG is a paying, nameable reference**

**Real, verified whitespace:** no vendor turns a live regulation into a company-specific DKK P&L band. Tamarind's "forecast" flagship is a ~$3,995 static Excel covering only vaping/pouches (zero cigars/pipe = ~85% of a combustibles maker); FiscalNote/CUBE stop at monitoring; Euromonitor is category-level; Oxford Economics is six-figure, months-long one-offs. The composite — reg in → entity-specific banded P&L with editable elasticity/pass-through + citation rails out — has **no incumbent**, and the one firm positioned to build it hired its first tech CEO in Feb 2026 and has shipped nothing AI-native. The window is open but closing.

**But it's a boutique, not a venture, for this founder, now:**
- **TAM:** ~10–40 serious buyers in tobacco/nicotine; honest niche SAM **DKK 50–150m/yr**; realistic solo 3-yr ceiling **DKK 2–6m ARR**. Adjacent regulated CPG (alcohol/HFSS-food/vaping/gambling/pharma) multiplies the category TAM into low-hundreds-of-millions DKK, but each needs its own data spine — a re-platforming bet, not free expansion.
- **The moat isn't the code** (replicable by anyone with Claude + the public corpus). It's (1) the curated, dated, cited corpus per category, (2) the honesty/citation/abstention discipline, (3) real-volume DPA access + the reference logo — none of which exist until you've sold once.
- **Funding:** do **not** raise and do **not** build a SaaS company now. Tobacco is VC-uninvestable, the buyer count is tiny, key-person risk too high, the engine too replicable for a venture moat. **Bootstrap entirely from STG/consulting cash.** It only becomes fundable — as "regulatory-impact intelligence for regulated CPG, tobacco as demo #1" — after 3–4 paying logos and a repeatable land-motion.
- **First moves (after STG is live):** run 3–5 discovery calls with **one other nicotine major** (PMI/BAT/JTI/Altria/Imperial — bigger budgets, identical EU-ETD/France/flavour exposure) **and one adjacent regulated category** (alcohol excise, HFSS/sugar-tax food, packaging/EPR). Only if **both** validate identical unserved pain, factor the reusable core (corpus + band engine + citation rails) into a thin single-tenant licensable version with a per-tenant config layer — so logo #2 is a config, not a rebuild. Keep services funding it; never build multi-tenancy before logo #2.

## 7. The hard truths that survived scrutiny (the Tenth Man)

- **The most likely single outcome is polite-meeting-then-death**: warm intro → one call → "impressive, send a proposal" → dies in a cost-cutting freeze with no champion. **Plan runway as if 2026 cash from this is DKK 0–150k (expected value)**; treat a signed pilot as upside. Don't quit other income.
- **The wow is a method, not an answer** — STG buys answers. **The DPA is the paywall and the whole game**: until real volumes are behind it, you're selling a beautifully-built *question*, and cost-cutting buyers don't pay for questions.
- **Your demo proves the value is buildable on public data** — handing an ex-BCG SVP the "why pay a solo, I'll task an analyst" objection. Counter by selling **method + speed + the real-data band they can't self-produce**, never the dashboard.
- **The IP clause is the whole asset.** Be willing to walk from a work-for-hire term even if it costs the deal — that term costs you the asset anyway.
- **Single-customer concentration is dangerous.** Convert pilot→retainer within ~6 months and land a 2nd independent buyer within 12 months so STG is **<60% of revenue**.
- **A wrong DKK figure to a CFO/board, or any drift toward investor-facing copy (MAR), is existential** — a regulatory incident traced to you, not a lost deal. Citation/abstention discipline + liability caps + E&O are mandatory.
- **The 90-day rule:** if there's no paid yes within ~90 days of first contact, the honest read is this is a **valent.dk portfolio/marketing piece, not a business** — stop building and move on.
