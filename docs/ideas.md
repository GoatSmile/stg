## 1. The strategic read — why STG, why now, why her

### The wedge the research document hands us

The document's own one-line synthesis is the entire pitch:

> "Technology is building the foundation, HR is imagining the future — but the
> organization still needs a unified story that connects OneProcess to
> AI-driven work transformation."

Nobody at STG owns the bridge. The CTO (Peter Schneider) owns the digital core
(OneProcess / SAP S/4HANA, 12 ERPs → 1). The HR Tech lead (Anne-Marie
Tørnes-Hansen) owns the future-of-work narrative ("AI is not a tech project —
it is a work transformation that HR must lead"). Between them is a vacuum, and
a vacuum is exactly where an outside builder-consultant can stand. **We don't
pitch a product. We pitch the bridge — made tangible as working software.**

### Who Anne-Marie actually is (two personas, one buyer)

- **Her LinkedIn persona** (per the source doc): visionary, provocative.
  Humans + AI agents working together; static org charts → dynamic data-driven
  models; HR as "orchestrator of intelligence, skills and experience";
  organizations fail at AI because they over-govern it, treat it like
  traditional IT, and limit experimentation.
- **Her operator persona** (per open-web research): Head of Global People Data
  and Digital Solutions. Co-led **PeopleHub** — STG's global SAP SuccessFactors
  rollout (employee data, performance, comp, succession, learning/STG Academy,
  analytics). On-time, on-budget. Doctrine: *"the job of HR is changing… now
  it's rather to ensure that we have high enough data quality."* Recently
  finished a Master's in IT Management (ITU Copenhagen). Reports into CHRO
  Thomas Kolber (since 2024).

The pitch must serve both personas: **visionary enough to match her public
voice, data-grounded enough to survive her operator instincts.** Vaporware
dies in the first meeting. A live demo on credible synthetic SuccessFactors-
shaped data is the move.

### Why now — four converging clocks

1. **Rethink! HR Tech Nordic, Copenhagen, 28–29 Sept 2026.** She has a speaker
   profile. That's ~3.5 months out. She needs a story to tell on stage. The
   strongest possible gift a consultant can hand her: *the case study she
   presents.* Build the prototype → pilot it quietly → she presents "how STG
   bridged ERP transformation and AI work redesign." We become the engine
   behind her conference moment.
2. **Cost pressure.** FY2025 was rough (organic sales −3.1%, adjusted EPS
   DKK 10.8 vs 13.7, SAP go-live disruptions). Focus2030 carries a ~DKK 200m
   cost program, ~DKK 100m landing in 2026. Every pitch must be cheap, fast,
   and self-evidently valuable. "I built this in days with AI" *is itself the
   proof of the method being sold.*
3. **Regulatory chaos in the growth engine.** Nicotine pouches are Focus2030
   priority #3 and the only real growth story (XQS >13% share in Sweden).
   Meanwhile: France banned pouches outright (effective March 2026, Conseil
   d'État merits ruling expected *this month*), Denmark capped at 9 mg +
   tobacco/menthol flavors only, Spain has a 0.99 mg proposal pending, Belgium/
   NL/LU/FI dark, EU TTD excise proposal deadlocked, TPD3 looming ~2028.
   Markets go dark with months of notice — that is a *workforce* event, not
   just a commercial one. HR has no tooling for it.
4. **SAP's roadmap is coming for her either way.** SAP Joule is shipping 30+
   AI use cases into SuccessFactors. If she does nothing, SAP defines her AI
   story for her, on SAP's timeline. A vendor-neutral bridge layer that makes
   *her* the orchestrator is the counter-move. Never pitch against SAP — pitch
   *ahead* of SAP.

### Honest caution (consultant's own decision)

This is the tobacco/nicotine industry. The work itself (HR, work redesign,
compliance tooling) is ethically clean, and STG's pouch story is framed as
harm reduction — but decide consciously that you're comfortable with the
client category before investing. Also: anything touching employment decisions
is **high-risk under the EU AI Act** — that's not a blocker, it's a
*credibility weapon* (see idea #18): walk in already fluent in what she'll be
audited on, while competitors hand-wave.

## 3. Solution portfolio

Four tiers: **vibe-coded quick strikes** (days), **product-grade prototypes**
(weeks), **Deloitte-style programs** (engagements), **moonshots** (outlandish
on purpose). Each idea tagged with the needs it hits.

### Tier 1 — vibe-coded quick strikes (1–5 days each, demoable)

**1. Work Redesign Studio** ⭐ *flagship candidate* — [N2, N4, N9]
Paste any STG job description (or pick a seeded role: financial controller,
customer service rep, demand planner, regulatory affairs specialist) → Claude
live-decomposes it into ~10–15 tasks → classifies each *automate / augment /
human-core* with reasoning → outputs a redesigned role card, a skills delta,
and a reskilling path framed around STG Academy. Aggregate view: an AI-impact
heatmap across a synthetic ~120-person org (SuccessFactors-shaped data).
*Why it wins:* it is her exact message, running live, on data shaped like her
own platform. Demo wow is the live decomposition of a role the audience knows.

**2. OneProcess → AI Opportunity Radar** — [N1, N8]
Interactive map of the four OneProcess value streams (Record-to-Report,
Source-to-Pay, Order-to-Cash, Plan-to-Deliver) × concrete AI use cases, each
scored on data-readiness (what S/4 now makes possible) and value. Click a
domain → see use cases, prerequisites, and which OneProcess capability unlocks
them. Pre-seeded content, no live AI needed. *Why it wins:* it literally draws
the bridge the document says is missing — and flatters the CTO's work instead
of competing with it (important for the joint CTO+HR room).

**3. AI Experiment Hub ("the greenhouse")** — [N3, N5, N8]
A lightweight internal portal: anyone submits an AI experiment idea (problem,
data, expected value), gets a governance-light triage (green/yellow/red using
EU AI Act risk logic), a sandbox prompt library, and a public leaderboard of
running experiments and harvested wins. *Why it wins:* it operationalizes her
anti-over-governance stance — governance as enablement rail, not gate.

**4. Pouch Regulatory Radar — with a workforce overlay** — [N6, N8]
Map of Europe, per-market pouch status (France: banned, possession
criminalized; Denmark: 9 mg cap + tobacco/menthol only; Spain: 0.99 mg
proposal; Sweden: open; EU: TTD excise deadlocked, TPD3 ~2028…), per-market
SKU compliance matrix (nicotine ceiling, flavor list, labeling, age limit),
and the twist nobody else demos: **click a market exit → see workforce
impact** — which synthetic roles/teams are exposed, redeployment options,
skills adjacency to surviving markets. *Why it wins:* connects HR to the
P&L conversation, and it's screamingly current (Conseil d'État ruling due
June 2026).

**5. PeopleHub Data-Quality Copilot** — [N4]
Upload (synthetic) SuccessFactors employee/position/skills extracts → AI scans
for the defects that kill AI projects: missing skills, stale job descriptions,
inconsistent titles, orphaned positions, duplicate competency labels →
prioritized fix-list with effort estimates. *Why it wins:* speaks directly to
her stated doctrine ("high enough data quality"). The least flashy and the
most her.

**6. "Ask P&C" agent demo** — [N5, N10]
A policy-RAG chat agent over (synthetic) STG HR policies and OneProcess change
FAQs, with citations, guardrails, escalation to humans, and an admin view
showing what employees actually ask (= demand signal for comms). The
Joule-adjacent demo, vendor-neutral.

**7. Change-comms translator** — [N10, N5]
Paste a corporate OneProcess announcement → generates role-specific "what
changes for me" versions (finance analyst vs. warehouse planner vs. sales
rep), in tone-of-voice, in da/en. Leaders are told to "cascade messages" —
this does the cascading. Tiny build, disproportionate delight.

**8. Skills extractor / JD modernizer** — [N2, N4]
Drop in STG's real public job postings → extract a normalized skills taxonomy
→ show overlap/gaps across postings → rewrite a JD in skills-based format.
Feeds idea #9; also a nice "we ran this on your own public data" hook.

### Tier 2 — product-grade prototypes (2–6 weeks)

**9. Skills Intelligence Layer** (TechWolf-lite on SuccessFactors) — [N2, N4, N7]
Infer skills from work artifacts and HR data; build the org-wide skills graph;
power redeployment, succession, and reskilling views. The natural "phase 2"
once the Work Redesign Studio lands. Buy-vs-build conversation included
(Gloat/Eightfold/TechWolf benchmark — see research brief §4).

**10. Dynamic Org Model — "from org chart to work graph"** — [N2, N9]
Her own words made software: a living graph where *work* (processes, projects,
tasks) connects to *capabilities* (human skills + AI agents), not boxes and
lines. Toggle: today's static chart ↔ the dynamic work-graph view of the same
synthetic org. Scenario slider: what shifts when X% of task-hours are
augmented. This is the most conference-stage-ready visual of everything here.

**11. Workforce Scenario Planner** — [N6, N5, N7]
Generalizes the regulatory radar: model market exit, factory wave go-live,
M&A integration (Mac Baren!), or cost-program scenarios → headcount, skills,
redeployment, severance vs. reskill cost curves. The CFO-friendly HR tool.

**12. AI Adoption Cockpit** — [N5, N3, N8]
Org-wide instrument panel: tool usage, literacy levels (self-assessed +
inferred), experiments running, value harvested, risk register. The artifact
a CHRO shows the board quarterly. Aggregates ideas #3 + #6 telemetry.

**13. Agentic HR service-delivery pilot** — [N5, N4]
Production-grade version of #6 scoped to one real process (e.g. onboarding
queries or leave policies), with proper evaluation, fallback, and audit
logging — built Joule-compatible so it's an *accelerant* of the SAP stack,
not a rival.

### Tier 3 — Deloitte-style consulting plays (programs, not apps)

**14. "The Bridge" — AI ambition & operating-model engagement** — [N1]
6–10 weeks: joint CTO/CHRO sponsorship; produce the unified narrative the
document says is missing — AI ambition statement, value-case portfolio per
OneProcess domain, operating model (hub/spoke), 18-month roadmap. The
prototype apps above serve as the *evidence base* that makes this engagement
feel earned rather than slideware.

**15. AI Center of *Enablement* design** — [N3, N5]
Deliberately not a "Center of Excellence" (centralized gatekeeping = the
over-governance she rails against). Design the federated model: central
rails (platform, legal, data), distributed experimentation (the greenhouse),
clear escalation tiers. Includes the experiment-funnel KPIs.

**16. AI literacy academy** — [N5]
Role-based curriculum wired into STG Academy (their existing SuccessFactors
learning module): executives, people leaders, specialists, frontline.
Persona-based, hands-on, measured. Partner-or-build comparison vs. Multiverse
/ Section / Sana.

**17. Future-of-work blueprint per OneProcess domain** — [N1, N2]
For each of R2R / S2P / O2C / P2D: post-OneProcess role inventory → AI-impact
analysis (using the Studio at scale) → target operating model per function →
transition plan. This is the "Work Redesign Studio sold as a program."

**18. Responsible-AI-by-design for HR (EU AI Act)** — [N3, N4]
Employment AI is high-risk under the EU AI Act. Build the lightweight
compliance kit: use-case register, risk classification flow, human-oversight
patterns, documentation templates — embedded into the Experiment Hub so
compliance is a property of the pipeline, not a committee. Walking in fluent
on this is a trust-builder no demo can match.

### Tier 4 — moonshots (outlandish on purpose)

**19. The first digital colleague — an AI agent with an employee file** — [N9, N3]
Onboard an AI agent through the *HR lifecycle itself*: it gets a PeopleHub-
style profile (job description, manager, objectives, probation period,
performance review), shows up in the org chart, and actually performs its job
live in the demo (e.g. answers policy questions, drafts change comms, watches
the regulatory radar). Performance review screen shows its KPIs and its
manager's feedback. *Why it's genius bait:* it is the literal, working
embodiment of her "humans + AI agents working together / HR as orchestrator
of intelligence" vision — and nobody else will dare demo it. Cheap to build
as theater, profound as provocation: if agents are workers, HR governs them.
That single thought repositions HR from support function to the control tower
of the AI era — *her* argument, won.

**20. STG organizational digital twin** — [N5, N6, N7]
Simulate the org: agents-as-employees model workload, skills, attrition;
replay the 2025 SAP go-live disruption and show what early-warning would have
caught; run the France-exit scenario forward. Decision-support theater of the
highest order.

**21. Craft Memory — saving the hands of the house** — [N11]
The sleeper hit. Handmade cigars are Focus2030 priority #2 and the artisan
workforce (rollers, blenders, graders in the Dominican Republic, Honduras,
Nicaragua) carries decades of tacit knowledge that retires with them.
Multimodal AI apprentice: video + interview capture of master artisans →
structured craft knowledge base → training companion for new rollers →
(provocation) a "master blender agent" that preserves a house palate forever.
Emotionally resonant, deeply on-brand, HR-owned (knowledge continuity is a
people problem), and an absolutely beautiful conference story. Nobody expects
an AI pitch to land in the rolling room.

**22. RitualCraft AI — generative consumer experience engine** *(adopted from
the Grok second-opinion report, see §6)* — [off the HR axis, deliberately]
AI agents that combine sales data + external trend/social signals to generate
personalized "ritual" recommendations for pouches/handmade cigars — flavor
pairings, virtual tasting sims, custom pouch designs; an "AI sommelier for
cigars." Not our pitch lane (Marketing/Product audience, and consumer-facing
AI in nicotine is a regulatory minefield — internal-only if ever), but worth
keeping as a leave-behind that shows range beyond HR.

**23. Leadership simulator — practice the hard conversations** — [N5, N9]
AI personas for rehearsing restructuring conversations, change resistance,
union consultations (Denmark!) — with feedback on empathy, clarity, legal
risk phrases. The DKK 200m cost program means hundreds of difficult
conversations are coming; nobody trains for them at scale.

**24. "Broen" immersive demo room** — [N1, N9]
For a leadership offsite: one wall shows an order flowing through OneProcess
(CTO's world), the other shows the humans + agents doing the work (her
world), and the bridge between them lights up as AI use cases activate.
Pure theater. Sometimes theater is what moves a board.

## 4. The recommended play — "Broen" (working title)

*Broen* — Danish for "The Bridge" — the Øresund bridge connects Denmark and
Sweden (a wink at the pouch geography), and the product bridges OneProcess ↔
future of work, CTO ↔ HR. One Next.js app, **three acts + a teaser**,
assembled from Tier 1 parts:

- **Act 1 — the foundation** (idea #2): OneProcess → AI Opportunity Radar.
  *"Your CTO built the foundation. Here's what it makes possible."*
- **Act 2 — the work** (idea #1, the centerpiece): Work Redesign Studio with
  live Claude decomposition + org-level heatmap.
  *"This is what 'AI is a work transformation' looks like as a tool."*
- **Act 3 — the future** (idea #19): the first digital colleague, complete
  with employee file and a live task.
  *"You said humans + agents, HR as orchestrator. Meet employee #00-AI-001."*
- **Teaser tile** (idea #4): the pouch regulatory radar with workforce
  overlay — the "and this is how HR earns a seat in the P&L conversation"
  parting shot, and the natural phase-2 engagement.

The meta-message, delivered explicitly at the end of the demo: **"This was
built by one person with AI in days, not by a vendor in quarters. That speed
is the capability I'm proposing STG builds — and it's exactly the
experimentation culture you've been writing about."** The medium is the
message.

## 5. Approach strategy for Anne-Marie

1. **Warm-up (now):** substantive engagement with her LinkedIn themes — not
   flattery, actual contributions (e.g. EU AI Act × HR experimentation; the
   "agents as employees" governance question). 2–3 weeks of presence before
   the ask.
2. **The ask:** short note — *"Your posts on AI as work transformation + what
   STG built with OneProcess made me build something. 25 minutes, I'll show
   you working software, no deck."* The no-deck promise is the differentiator.
3. **The meeting:** run Broen. Let her drive Act 2 with a role she chooses.
   Close with two concrete offers (both adopted from the Grok report, §6):
   a joint **AI Experimentation Lab co-owned by her team** (our idea #3 with
   her name on it), and an offer to **facilitate the CTO alignment session**
   — we become the bridge-builder in person, not just in software.
4. **The conference play:** offer the case-study path — quiet pilot over the
   summer → her Rethink! HR Tech Nordic story (28–29 Sept, Copenhagen).
   Position as *her* story; we're the engine.
5. **Expansion path:** Act 2 → skills layer pilot (#9) → "The Bridge"
   engagement (#14) with joint CTO sponsorship → cockpit (#12) as the
   retained relationship.
6. **Framing disciplines:** never against SAP (we're the vendor-neutral glue
   ahead of Joule's roadmap); never "AI strategy" (she owns the vision — we
   make it tangible); always synthetic data until a DPA exists (and say so —
   it signals AI Act fluency, which *is* the trust moment).

## 6. Second opinion — the Grok report (assessed June 2026)

A parallel ideation was run through Grok ("STG AI Transformation Ideation:
Bridging OneProcess Foundation to AI-Driven Work & Business Reinvention",
source PDF in the owner's project folder, not in this repo). Verdict:
**useful as convergent validation and for one strategic upgrade (Joule
fluency), but its factual claims needed correction before client use** —
we fact-checked the load-bearing ones (results below and in
[research-brief.md](research-brief.md) §5).

### Where it agrees (independent convergence = thesis validation)
Same wedge ("position AI as the next layer on OneProcess, not a separate
tech project"), same buyer ("she is the ideal entry point"), same flagship
shape (its "AI-Powered Skills & Role Redesign Simulator" ≈ our Work Redesign
Studio, idea #1, down to the 1-week-prototype estimate), same experimentation
lab (its "PeopleHub 2.0" ≈ our Greenhouse, #3), same unified-narrative move
(§3 of its report ≈ our "Broen" framing). Two LLMs ideating independently
from the same source landed on the same spine — that's signal, not
coincidence.

### What we adopted
- **Joule fluency as a layer on Act 1.** Its strongest strategic point:
  meet STG *inside* their SAP reality. Each radar use-case card now carries a
  "Joule covers this today / custom bridge wins here" badge (spec updated).
  We stay vendor-neutral but demonstrably Joule-literate — that's more
  credible than ignoring SAP's roadmap.
- **Two pitch-close beats** (folded into §5): the joint AI Experimentation
  Lab *co-owned by her team*, and the explicit offer to facilitate the CTO
  alignment session.
- **Named-agent specificity** for radar content (e.g. an "order reliability"
  use case in O2C) — concrete names make the radar feel researched, not
  generated.
- **RitualCraft AI** added as moonshot #22 (consumer-side range, with the
  regulatory caveat).

### What we rejected, and why
- **"Feed anonymized OneProcess/HR data via secure API" for a cold-pitch
  prototype.** Disqualifying for a first meeting — receiving client HR data
  pre-DPA is a GDPR landmine and would torpedo the AI-Act-fluency trust
  moment. Synthetic data is not a compromise, it *is* the pitch discipline.
- **Streamlit/LangGraph MVP stack.** Right for an internal PoC, wrong for a
  pitch — a Streamlit app reads as a science project; the polish of a real
  product is itself evidence of the "vibe-coding velocity" claim. We keep
  Next.js/shadcn.
- **The "conspiratorial angle" (AI as invisible workforce layer, "many firms
  already doing this quietly").** Tone-deaf for an HR leader whose brand is
  *safe, transparent* experimentation — quiet-AI framing is the exact
  opposite of her message.
- **Its 15-minute meeting carrying two demos + a lab proposal + a CTO
  session offer** — overstuffed; we keep 25–30 min, one demo, two closes.
- **What it missed entirely** (our differentiators stand): the conference
  angle (Rethink! HR Tech Nordic, Sept 2026), the EU AI Act high-risk lever,
  the regulation→workforce overlay, Craft Memory, and any awareness of her
  operator persona (data-quality doctrine, PeopleHub history).

### Fact-check of its load-bearing claims (June 2026, sourced in research-brief §5)
| Grok claim | Verdict | Reality to use instead |
|---|---|---|
| "Joule Studio low-code agent builder, GA 2026; GA agents for finance/supply chain/HR/procurement" | Partially true | Skill builder GA Jul 2025, agent builder GA Dec 2025/Jan 2026; ≥1 GA agent per domain but much of the portfolio still beta; Joule embedded in S/4HANA **Cloud** + SuccessFactors (not on-prem). A *new* managed "Joule Studio" (announced Sapphire May 2026) is NOT yet GA |
| "30–80% time savings on routine tasks (SAP benchmarks)" | Conflation | SAP's actual claims: 2,100+ automations covering ~80% of most-used transactions, routine tasks "up to 80% faster", a *goal* of 30% productivity — all vendor-indicative, none audited |
| "Pouch market CAGR ~36%" | Top-end cherry-pick | Consensus 25–30% (Grand View 24.7–29.6%, TBRC ~28.9%, Persistence 28.4%); 36% exists only at the optimistic tail (SkyQuest 36.3%) |
| "Vibe-code custom skills in BTP (Python/LangChain wrappers)"; "SAP Build Apps" | Stale/imprecise | **SAP Build Apps retired as standalone March 23, 2026** (folded into SAP Build). Joule skills are declarative low-code, not LangChain wrappers; the pro-code path is SAP Cloud SDK for AI (`sap-ai-sdk-gen`) on BTP, reaching S/4HANA via standard APIs |

Meta-lesson, worth saying in the pitch room if AI tooling comes up: *both*
reports were AI-generated and converged on strategy, but the uncited one
shipped stale product facts and cherry-picked numbers — which is precisely
why the Experiment Hub (#3) pairs enablement with verification rails. Our
own discipline (every claim dated + sourced) is a live demo of responsible
AI practice.
