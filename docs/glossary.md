# Glossary — plain-language terms

*Updated: 2026-06-22 09:59 CEST*

*Quick, plain-English definitions of the finance, market, regulatory, HR and tech terms used
across the app and docs — written for a non-finance reader. Skim it, or Ctrl-F a term. STG figures
are illustrative anchors, not live.*

---

## Money & profit (reading a P&L)

- **Net sales (revenue)** — total money from selling product, before any costs. STG ≈ DKK 9bn/yr.
- **Organic growth** — sales growth with currency swings *and* acquisitions stripped out, so you see the *underlying* business. ("Reported" growth includes those; "organic" is the cleaner number.)
- **Gross margin** — what's left of sales after the direct cost of making the product, as a %.
- **EBIT** — *Earnings Before Interest & Tax* = operating profit, i.e. profit from running the business before financing costs and tax.
- **EBIT margin** — EBIT ÷ sales, as a %. "Operating profit per krone of sales." STG ~14–15%.
- **EBITDA** — EBIT with depreciation & amortisation (paper costs for wear/intangibles) added back — a rough proxy for operating cash. **b.s.i.** = "before special items."
- **EBITDA margin** — EBITDA ÷ sales, %.
- **Special items** — one-off, non-recurring costs (restructuring, the SAP project) shown separately so they don't muddy "normal" profit.
- **Net profit** — the bottom line: what's left after *everything* — costs, interest, tax. STG FY2025 DKK 669m.
- **Contribution margin** — sales minus the *variable* cost of those sales; what each extra krone of sales "contributes" toward fixed costs + profit. (The Impact Room multiplies lost revenue by this.)
- **FCF (free cash flow)** — actual cash left after running and investing in the business — the cash you can pay dividends/debt with. *Profit ≠ cash; FCF is the cash.*
- **EPS (earnings per share)** — net profit ÷ number of shares.
- **ROIC (return on invested capital)** — profit earned per krone of capital put into the business, %. A core efficiency gauge. STG ~7.9%, targeting ≥11% by 2030.
- **Leverage** — how indebted the company is (net debt ÷ EBITDA). STG 3.0x, target 2.5x. Higher = riskier.
- **Dividend / payout ratio** — dividend = cash paid to shareholders per share; payout ratio = the % of profit paid out as dividend.
- **FP&A** — *Financial Planning & Analysis*: the in-house finance team that does budgeting/forecasting.

## Stock-market words

- **Market cap** — total value of all the shares (share price × shares outstanding).
- **Guidance** — the company's own forecast of its coming results. (**Guidance credibility** = whether investors believe it, based on its hit/miss track record.)
- **P/E (price-to-earnings)** — share price ÷ EPS; roughly how many years of profit you pay for the share. Lower = cheaper.
- **P/B (price-to-book)** — share price vs net asset value per share.
- **Dividend yield** — dividend ÷ share price, %; the "interest-like" return from dividends.
- **TSR (total shareholder return)** — share-price change + dividends; the all-in return to a holder.
- **CAGR (compound annual growth rate)** — the smoothed average growth-per-year over several years. "Pouches grow ~28% CAGR" = ~28% a year.

## STG's segments & products

- **EUB / NABROW / NAOR** — STG's three divisions: **E**urope **B**randed · **N**orth **A**merica **B**randed **& R**est-**o**f-**W**orld · **N**orth **A**merica **O**nline **& R**etail (the Cigars International web shops + superstores).
- **HMC** — *handmade cigars* (premium, hand-rolled; US-centric — the profit engine).
- **MRC** — *machine-rolled cigars* (mass-produced cigars/cigarillos; the European cash cow).
- **NGP** — *next-generation products* = STG's term for **nicotine pouches** (the XQS brand).
- **SKU (stock-keeping unit)** — one specific product variant (a given flavour + strength + pack size). "Delists 23 SKUs" = removes 23 specific variants.
- **XQS / ZYN / VELO / Nordic Spirit** — nicotine-pouch brands: XQS is STG's; ZYN (PMI), VELO (BAT), Nordic Spirit (JTI) are the big rivals.
- **RSP / WAP** — *retail selling price* / *weighted average price* — price bases excise rules use (tax = X% of RSP or WAP).

## The Impact Room's assumptions (the sliders)

- **Exposed share** — the slice of STG's base a regulation actually hits (you set it).
- **Pass-through** — how much of a tax rise is passed to the shopper vs absorbed by STG.
- **Elasticity** — how much demand falls when price rises (price sensitivity).
- **Recapture** — for a ban/cap: how much lost revenue returns via compliant substitute products.
- **Band (low / base / high)** — the answer as a *range*, not one number — the honest expression of uncertainty.

## Regulation

- **Excise** — a tax governments levy specifically on tobacco/nicotine (per unit or % of price). The thing the EU revision raises.
- **ETD / Tobacco Taxation Directive — COM(2025) 580** — the EU proposal to raise *minimum excise* across tobacco and pouches. (COM(…) = a numbered European Commission proposal.) The worked example in the app.
- **TPD3** — the *separate* EU Tobacco **Products** Directive track: flavours, packaging, plain-packaging rules — **not** taxes. (Keep ETD = tax, TPD3 = product rules.)
- **MAR (Market Abuse Regulation)** — EU rules barring selective/forward-looking info that could move a listed company's price; why the app is strictly internal-use, never investor-facing.
- **GDPR / DPA** — EU data-privacy law / the *data-processing agreement* signed before any real STG data is handled.
- **TRIS / EUR-Lex** — EU databases of draft and enacted laws (the future live regulatory feed).
- **FDA / PMTA** — US Food & Drug Administration / its pre-market authorisation process for nicotine products.

## HR, operations & supply

- **Headcount** — number of employees. **Attrition / turnover** — the rate they leave and need replacing.
- **SuccessFactors / RMK** — the SAP human-resources/recruiting software STG runs; the source of the live careers/jobs data.
- **ERP / SAP S/4HANA / OneProcess** — ERP = the big system running finance, supply and operations; *S/4HANA* is SAP's product; *OneProcess* is STG's project merging 12 old ERPs into one (the disruption behind the 2025 cash-flow miss).
- **OTIF (on-time-in-full)** — a supply-chain quality metric: deliveries arriving on time *and* complete.
- **Leaf** — raw tobacco leaf (the farmed input; grown/processed in Indonesia, Sri Lanka, the Dominican Republic).
- **Freight** — the cost of shipping goods (the app uses a Brent-crude signal as a proxy for it).
- **Water stress / WRI Aqueduct / ENSO** — environmental risk to leaf-growing regions: *WRI Aqueduct* = a water-scarcity dataset; *ENSO* = the El Niño/La Niña climate cycle that swings rainfall.

## A few tech terms (if a CTO asks)

- **Citation rail** — the code that checks every number the AI states against a cited source, and rejects (abstains) anything that doesn't match.
- **Abstention** — when the model says *"not stated in the source"* instead of guessing.
- **Golden response** — a pre-written, human-reviewed answer served when the AI is offline, so the demo never depends on wifi.
- **SSO / RLS** — *single sign-on* (corporate login) / *row-level security* (who-can-read-which-rows in the database).
