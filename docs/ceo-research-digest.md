BRIEF: NIELS FREDERIKSEN (CEO, Scandinavian Tobacco Group, CPH:STG) — PUBLIC STATEMENTS, ANALYST PRESSURE, STOCK CONTEXT, 2025–2026
(Compiled 2026-06-12. Frederiksen: CEO since May 2018, internal promotion; CFO is Marianne Rørslev Bock; IR: Torben Sand; Chairman: Henrik Brandt.)

═══ 1. TIMELINE OF KEY EVENTS & WHAT HE SAID ═══

**Q1 2025 report (20 May 2025) — first guidance cut.** Organic net sales -8.8% (US handmade cigar drop + ZYN distribution change); reported +1.3% to DKK 2.0bn (Mac Baren acquisition). CEO quote: "We are adjusting our full year expectations to reflect the impact of a weaker USD and increasing tariffs on imported goods to the US." Framing: "underlying business trends remain largely unchanged… main priority being to protect and enhance market shares and protect cash flow." Tariff price increases alone expected to cost slightly over 0.5pp of EBITDA margin. **Q2 2025 (27 Aug 2025).** Organic -4.1%; EBITDA margin 21.1% vs 24.5%. Quote: "Despite the challenging market environment driven by tariffs and geopolitical unrest, I am pleased that we have remained focused on delivering on our strategic priorities." Margin drags: mix, investment to regain European machine-rolled share, ZYN discontinuation. XQS pouches double-digit growth. Guidance reaffirmed (FCF DKK 800–1,000m). ([GlobeNewswire Q2 2025](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/08/27/3140200/0/en/Scandinavian-Tobacco-Group-A-S-Reports-Second-Quarter-2025-Results-and-Reaffirms-Expectations-for-Full-Year.html))

([GlobeNewswire Q3 2025](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/11/11/3185639/0/en/Scandinavian-Tobacco-Group-A-S-Reports-Third-Quarter-2025-Results-and-Narrows-Expectation-Ranges-for-Full-Year.html))

**Capital Markets Day (20 Nov 2025; ambitions released 19 Nov) — "Focus2030" five-year strategy.** Quote: "We are excited to launch our Focus2030 strategy, and we are confident that it will create value for consumers, employees and shareholders. Even more importantly, we are creating a company that is even better positioned to drive value beyond 2030." Three pillars for 2030: (1) sustainable/stable machine-rolled cigars + smoking tobacco focused on Europe; (2) growing handmade cigar business anchored in US with stronger global footprint; (3) bigger nicotine pouch business. Financial ambitions: ROIC ≥11% by end-2030 (vs 7.8–7.9% now); low single-digit organic EBIT CAGR (before special items); FCF ≥DKK 1.2bn in 2030; ~DKK 200m cost-efficiency program. NEW shareholder return policy: dividend payout 40–60% of adjusted EPS + buybacks only when projected leverage permits vs 2.5x target — replacing the prior stable/rising dividend + perennial buyback model (>DKK 9bn returned since 2016 IPO). Market share targets: machine-rolled US <27%→>29%; handmade US ~13%→>15%. ([GlobeNewswire CMD ambitions](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/11/19/3190642/0/en/Scandinavian-Tobacco-Group-announces-financial-ambitions-and-new-flexible-shareholder-return-policy-ahead-of-Capital-Markets-Day.html); [CMD page + deck](https://www.st-group.com/investor/capital-markets-day-2025/); [Seeking Alpha CMD transcript (paywalled)](https://seekingalpha.com/article/4846177-scandinavian-tobacco-group-a-s-stbgy-analyst-investor-day-transcript))

- **Sebastian Grave (Nordea):** underlying margin pressure vs one-offs; Mac Baren synergy realization (~DKK 150m by 2027 per consensus narratives); timing of the DKK 200m efficiency program — answer: ~DKK 100m lands 2026, remainder 2027–28.
- **Damian McNeela (Deutsche Numis):** Canada licensing restrictions and Australia illicit-trade profit headwinds; competitive dynamics in Europe post-SAP disruption; XQS expansion strategy.

Q1 2026 call (21 May 2026):

- **McNeela (Deutsche Numis):** European MRC recovery and SAP leverage; nature of online competition beyond price (free-shipping war in US online cigar retail compressing handmade margins); XQS rollout into other EU markets.

Recurring themes management is grilled on: credibility of guidance (Danish press: STG "disappointed in 6 of the last 8 quarters" — [TradeDesk](https://tradedesk.dk/stock/stg.co)); FCF reliability post-SAP; leverage 3.0x vs 2.5x promise; dividend-cut logic; why CMD ambitions diverged from 2026 guidance within ~14 weeks; SAP data reliability — Devin LaSarre (Invariant, 31 May 2026) questions whether reported margin improvements are trustworthy given ERP disruption, and criticizes the failed prior "Rolling Towards 2025" strategy ([Invariant](https://invariant.substack.com/p/scandinavian-tobacco-group-what-if)).

═══ 5. WHAT HE EMPHASIZES / WORRIES ABOUT / PROMISES ═══

Emphasizes: market-share stabilization ("one quarter does not change a trend"), handmade +8% organic, XQS momentum (55% organic growth FY25; Sweden 13.6%), margin improvement from cost program, FCF normalization in 2026, Focus2030 discipline.
Worries (his own words): US tariffs, weak US consumer/trading down, USD depreciation (5.2% Q1-26 headwind), "geopolitical uncertainty… a market condition," fierce online price competition in US handmade (free shipping), Australia illicit trade, Canada licensing, SAP execution risk (US rollout late 2026 — next operational landmine), "structurally declining category."
Promises on record: leverage to 2.5x by end-2026; FCF DKK 950–1,200m in 2026 and ≥1.2bn by 2030; ROIC ≥11% by 2030; low single-digit EBIT CAGR; DKK 200m cost savings (~100m in 2026); MRC share >29% and handmade >15% by 2030; nicotine pouch business ≥DKK 1bn revenue; dividend 40–60% payout maintained.

═══ THE 5 QUESTIONS THE CEO IS MOST UNDER PRESSURE TO ANSWER RIGHT NOW ═══

1. **Credibility gap: why should investors believe Focus2030 when 2026 guidance broke the CMD ambitions within ~3 months and STG missed its own narrowed FY25 targets?** (Ekman pressed exactly this; stock halved; 6-of-8-quarter disappointment record.)
2. **Cash and the 2.5x promise: will leverage actually hit 2.5x by end-2026 and FCF land in the 950–1,200m range — i.e., did the SAP receivables really come back — and when do buybacks resume?** (FCF missed by >200m in 2025; Q1-26 leverage still 3.0x; buybacks suspended for the first time since the IPO era.)
3. **Structural decline vs growth story: handmade US consumption is falling mid-single digits and online price wars are eating margins — what is the actual mechanism for "revitalizing" handmade cigars beyond cost cuts and share-point targets?** (His own admission: "difficult consumer environment in a structurally declining category with fierce competition.")
4. **Nicotine pouches: how does a sub-scale #4 player ("larger among the smaller players") build a ≥DKK 1bn pouch business against PMI/Swedish Match — and what is the US entry path given he admits "could we launch in the US today? The answer is no" (no FDA authorization)?** (Ekman also flagged the reported -23% NGP organic sales in Q1-26 vs the growth narrative.)
5. **Execution/self-inflicted wounds: after the European SAP rollout disrupted sales, receivables and possibly the reliability of reported numbers, what guarantees that the late-2026 US SAP deployment won't trigger a repeat — and are the reported margin improvements real or ERP noise?** (LaSarre/Invariant explicitly questions data reliability; analysts probe "SAP leverage" claims.)

# Scandinavian Tobacco Group (STG) — Commercial Footprint Research Brief
Compiled 2026-06-12. FX context: ~DKK 6.4–6.9/USD during 2025; FY = calendar year. Primary sources: STG Annual Report 2025 (published 2026-03-04) and Interim Report Q4 2025 (2026-03-04), both parsed from original PDFs.

## 1. Group financials (FY2025, reported 4 Mar 2026)
- Net sales DKK 9,035.7m (FY24: 9,202.1m); reported -1.8%, organic -3.1% (-1.8% excluding discontinued distribution of third-party ZYN pouches in US online). Q4 2025 missed even the guidance lowered on 11 Nov 2025 (DKK 9.1–9.2bn); shares plunged on results (Investing.com earnings call transcript, 4 Mar 2026).
- EBITDA before special items DKK 1,791m, margin 19.8% (FY24: DKK 2,079m, 22.6%). Gross margin 44%.
- New strategy "Focus2030" launched with FY25 results: (1) Win with handmade cigars in the US, (2) Lead in machine-rolled cigars in Europe, (3) Build a competitive nicotine pouch business in Europe. Power Brands: HMC = Macanudo, CAO, Cohiba, Alec Bradley; MRC = Signature, La Paz, Mehari's, Panter; pouches = XQS only.
- Q1 2026 (reported 20 May 2026): net sales DKK 1.9bn, -0.6% at constant FX (FX -5.2%); HMC organic +8%; NGP organic ~-23% (ACE/GRITT footprint rationalization; NGP gross margin 23%→35.2% YoY); EBITDA margin b.s.i. 17.2% (Q1'25: 16.1%). FY26 guidance: constant-FX net sales -2% to +2%, EBITDA ~flat vs 2025, FCF DKK 950–1,200m, adj. EPS DKK 9–11.
- Scale: ~8,800 employees, ~200 brands, products in ~100 markets, >10 manufacturing sites. HMC made in Dominican Republic, Honduras, Nicaragua; MRC in Europe (Belgium/NL), DR, Indonesia; pipe/fine-cut in Denmark + Richmond, VA (Mac Baren); pouches in Denmark, Sweden, Poland.

## 3. Division detail
**Europe Branded** (own sales organisations in 12 markets: DE, DK, SE, FR, IT, BE, NL, LU, ES, PT, UK, IE; sells to wholesalers, distributors and direct-to-retail). Mix: MRC 55%, smoking tobacco 27%, NGP 12% (≈DKK 390m — nearly all of STG's pouch revenue sits here), HMC 4%. Largest markets: **France 19% (~DKK 620m), Germany 18% (~DKK 590m), Sweden 12% (~DKK 390m)** of divisional sales. Gross margin 48% (highest), but highest OPEX ratio (own sales orgs everywhere).

**NABROW** (wholesalers/distributors supplying retail in US+Canada, plus Australia, Middle East, Africa, South America, Asia, "International Sales" incl. Norway/Finland/Switzerland/Israel, Global Travel Retail, contract manufacturing & accessories). Mix: HMC 33%, smoking tobacco 27%, MRC 22%, Other/CMA 16%, NGP 2%. Largest markets: **USA 50% (~DKK 1.5bn), Canada 7%, Norway 6%**. International (ex-US) handmade ~8% of division, almost tripled in 4 years. Profit engine: 31.1% EBITDA margin but down from 36.0% — Q4 hit by steep total-market declines in Australia and Canada, "two of our most profitable markets". US: market leader in both pipe tobacco (Captain Black) and fine-cut (Bugler); Canada: MRC market leader; Norway: fine-cut leader (Tiedemanns).

**NAOR** (direct-to-consumer; all US): **88% online/catalogue (~DKK 2.42bn ≈ USD ~350m) / 12% retail (~DKK 330m)**. This is the de facto disclosed e-commerce number. Websites: CigarsInternational.com (largest), ThompsonCigar.com (acquired 2018; Tampa heritage mail-order, est. 1915), Cigar.com, CigarBid.com (auction site, founded by Keith Meier; CigarFest event in PA also his creation), PipesAndCigars.com. ~1m active online consumers and 25m catalogues mailed across five websites (2021 figure, st-group.com facts page; catalogue volumes being cut for cost/ESG). 12-month active consumer file: -3% YoY in Q4 2025, -1% QoQ — customer acquisition impaired by promo wars and total-market decline; retention solid. NAOR also distributes third-party brands (distributed PMI's ZYN until discontinued mid-2024 — its loss drove the -17% NGP organic and NAOR's decline in H1 2025). Exclusive proprietary brands sold only via NAOR: Man O'War, 5 Vegas. **Retail: 15 Cigars International Superstores at end-2025** (+2 in 2025: Newport, KY #14 Nov 2025; Orlando, FL #15 Dec 2025/Jan 2026 grand opening), all profitable; locations across PA (Bethlehem x2, Hamburg, South Fayette), TX (The Colony — largest store, rebuilt 2025; Fort Worth area), FL (Jacksonville, Tampa, Orlando), TN, KY, plus planned Chesterfield, MO (halfwheel/Cigar Snob, Nov 2025–Jan 2026). NA Retail organic ~+7% in Q4 2025 (store openings; same-store sales slightly down); HMC via superstores +15% FY25. Meier & Dutch = STG's US wholesale distributor (Bethlehem, PA) serving thousands of B2B retail accounts; third-party estimate ~USD 23.2m revenue (ZoomInfo, undated, low confidence — not separately disclosed by STG). ## THE 3 SHARPEST GAPS

1. **Regulation→P&L quantification and scenario modeling does not exist as a product, anywhere.** Tamarind's "forecasting" flagship is a $3,995 static Excel emailed within 24h; horizon-scanning AI tools explicitly stop at monitoring/summarizing (no financial impact, no scenarios — stated in category literature, Feb 2026); quantitative excise models exist only as public-health tools (TETSiM, Tobacconomics) or six-figure bespoke consulting (Oxford Economics). An AI-native engine that takes a live regulatory event (e.g., ETD minimums, a state pouch-tax bill) and outputs probability-weighted EBITDA/volume/price-elasticity scenarios per market per SKU portfolio has zero incumbent.

2. **No entity-aware, materiality-scored alerting.** Daily alerts exist (Tamarind) but every subscriber gets the same country-headline stream; nothing maps an alert against a specific company's footprint to answer "is this Moldova consultation worth €0 or €20M to *us*, and by when." The triage layer between "alert" and "board slide" is 100% human today, at every nicotine company.

3. **STG's core categories are an orphaned coverage zone.** The only specialist vendor (Tamarind) covers vaping/heated/pouches and **excludes cigars, pipe, RYO entirely**; generic platforms (FiscalNote, CUBE, Regology) are US/finserv-weighted and not category-fluent; public databases lag and carry a tobacco-control framing. A unified combustibles+NGP global regulatory/excise data layer — structured, API/agent-queryable rather than portal/PDF/Excel — has no incumbent, and the one company positioned to build it (Tamarind) only installed its first tech CEO in Feb 2026 and has shipped nothing AI-native yet.

**Pricing dynamics**: EU online prices €0.13-0.25/pouch mainstream, outlet/discount €0.07-0.12; online typically 30-50% below brick-and-mortar (thesnusoutlet.com price breakdown, 2026; snusdaddy.com price guide) [LOW-MED]. ## 3. ILLICIT TRADE / PARALLEL IMPORTS POST-FRENCH BAN

- **France legal status [HIGH]**: Decree no. 2025-898 published 2025-09-05 banning all oral nicotine products (pouches, beads, gums, lozenges) except medicines. Conseil d'État interim ruling 2025-12-22 (case brought by manufacturer EVLB): suspended the decree ONLY as regards **manufacture, production and export** (court said firm needed until end-June 2026 to relocate production); ban on **sale, distance sale, import, possession and use** entered into force 2026-04-01. Merits ruling expected by ~June 2026 (franceinfo.fr, Dec 2025; leclubdesjuristes.com; service-public.gouv.fr A18450; se.diplomatie.gouv.fr). Penalties: up to €15,000 fine + 1 year prison for possession/use; customs empowered to seize and destroy (nicotine-pouches.org ban explainer; stripes.com 2026-06-05 — US military paper warning personnel in Europe, confirming active enforcement salience in June 2026). BAT France confirmed compliance/halt of sales from April 2026 (2firsts.com).
- **Gray-market evidence**: French customs ramped parcel seizures through 2025 ahead of the ban (ordernicotinepouches.com France page [LOW-MED]); post-ban, multiple EU retailers openly continue shipping to France, and user reports indicate small orders (1-2 cans) frequently get through (darepouch.com France 2026 guide; ordernicotinepouches.com) [LOW-MED but consistent across sources]. French commentators and industry (incl. Philip Morris France) predicted parallel/cross-border markets from Germany/Spain/Italy/Luxembourg (nicotineworld.fr, 2025-12-03; smoking.fr). Precedent cited repeatedly: French disposable-vape ("puff") ban — products remained easily available via parallel channels (ginn.global, 2025).
- **Precedent markets [HIGH/MED]**: Belgium banned pouch sales Oct 2023; Netherlands banned sales 2025-01-01 (NVWA enforces; possession legal; Dutch buyers documented ordering from EU sites) — both show ban→cross-border-online substitution pattern (generationsanstabac.org; business.gov.nl; tobaccointelligence.com Belgium Dec 2025). Norway moving to ban cross-border online nicotine sales entirely (northerner.com news) — the loophole-closing countermove to watch. No published official seizure statistics specific to pouches found in FR/BE/NL — data gap; French Douanes annual report would be next primary source to check.
- **Macro point for pitch**: EU pouch regulation is fragmenting (FR/BE/NL bans; DK 9mg+flavour cap; ES proposed 0.99mg cap; PL proposed flavour ban + online sales ban; DE/IT/SE/CZ open), while the product ships in an envelope. Structural arbitrage between regulated/banned and open markets is growing, and nobody publishes systematic measurement of it.

## 4. FEASIBILITY OF MONITORING ONLINE RETAIL PRICES/LISTINGS ACROSS EU

- **Technically easy and precedented.** nicotine-pouches.org already crawls 10+ shops daily and sells an API. Target sites are standard e-commerce (mostly Shopify/Magento-class) with clean product pages: price, strength (mg/pouch and mg/g), flavor, format, pack count, stock, ratings, bestseller sort.
- **Site inventory for a monitor**: Haypp Group properties (haypp.com /uk /se /de /at + .com US, northerner.com multi-country, snusbolaget.se, nettotobak.com), Snusdaddy (EU+UK), Snusdirect.eu, Europesnus.com, Whitepouches.com/eu, Snuzone, SnusMe.eu, plus UK vape chains (ukecigstore.com, theecig.co.uk) and per-market players (PL: local shops despite formal online-sale ban — itself a compliance signal worth monitoring; IT: tobacconist e-commerce). Country-site matrix gives per-market price/assortment/launch detection, incl. who ships into banned markets (FR/BE/NL checkout tests = gray-market flow indicator).
- **robots.txt verified for haypp.com [HIGH]**: only internal search pages disallowed; 34 sitemaps listed (products, brands, categories, articles, per country) — i.e., full product catalogs are deliberately exposed for crawling; no crawl-delay. Sitemap-diffing alone gives launch/delist detection without HTML scraping.
- **Legal/ToS considerations**: robots-permitted crawling of public price data is low-risk under EU law (no GDPR issue for product data; database-right risk minimal for facts like prices; post-*Ryanair v PR Aviation* ToS can contractually restrict scraping of non-protected databases, so check each retailer's ToS; rate-limit politely). Buying Haypp Media & Insights data is the fully clean alternative/complement. Age-gates are simple interstitials on most sites (a compliance-relevant observation in itself).
- **What a monitor yields**: daily price-per-pouch index by brand/market (track ZYN premium vs Velo vs XQS mid-price positioning), promo intensity, new SKU/flavor launch detection (e.g., VELO Shift, ZYN 16.5mg appeared online first), stock-outs, bestseller-rank movements (Haypp exposes "bestsellers" sort = near-real-time share proxy), regulatory compliance drift (9mg+ SKUs still listed on DK site after 2026-04-01? flavored SKUs shipped to PL?), and cross-border shipping matrices into FR/BE/NL.
